Microcontroller Board Design & Fabrication

End-to-end development of a functional Nuvoton N76E003 microcontroller board using KiCad.

Project Overview

This project focused on building a complete microcontroller board from scratch, covering schematic design, PCB layout, fabrication, soldering, and final testing. The objective was to understand the full hardware development cycle and produce a reliable, working board.

The selected microcontroller was the Nuvoton N76E003, an 8051-based IC widely used in industrial applications. The board was successfully programmed and tested using the Keil µVision IDE and Nu-Link programmer.

Key Information

Timeline: June 2025 – July 2025

Mentor: Mr. Navlesh Gawale

Team Size: 4 Members

Skills Used:

KiCad PCB Design Soldering Keil µVision Gerber Generation

Design & Fabrication Workflow

1. Schematic & PCB Layout

Designed the complete schematic and PCB layout in KiCad. After verification and DRC checks, generated the required Gerber files for fabrication.

View KiCad Files
2. Physical Fabrication

Fabrication included laminating, etching, drilling, applying solder mask, and silkscreen printing. Components were soldered with precision to avoid short circuits and ensure reliability.

3. Testing & Programming

Used the Nu-Link programmer along with Keil µVision IDE to upload and test firmware. Successfully executed UART communication and LED blinking programs.
