Competitive FPV Drone Design & Integration

End-to-end build of 3" Freestyle and 5" Race Class FPV drones used in national competitions.

Project Overview

This project involved building high-performance First Person View (FPV) drones for both freestyle and racing categories. The goal was to create robust and agile drones capable of competing at inter-college and national-level events.

Work included frame assembly, precise soldering, flight controller setup, firmware tuning, battery optimization, and pre-flight testing. Special focus was given to reliable electrical connections and responsive flight performance.

Engineering Contributions

1. Hardware Selection & Integration

Selected and integrated components such as motors, ESCs, flight controllers, FPV camera, VTX, and antennas based on drone class. Ensured correct wiring, low-noise power distribution, and safe soldering to prevent mid-air failure.

2. Flight Firmware Setup

Configured and tuned Betaflight and INAV firmware. Adjusted PID values, filtering, and motor output limits to achieve stable, responsive performance suitable for racing and freestyle use.
